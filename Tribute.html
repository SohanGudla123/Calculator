Rabindranath Tagore

Rabindranath Tagore (1861–1941) was a Bengali poet, philosopher, and polymath who reshaped Indian literature and music. Best known for his Nobel Prize-winning work Gitanjali, Tagore’s poetry, stories, and songs continue to inspire millions. He was a visionary who founded Visva-Bharati University and championed universal humanism.

Timeline of Achievements

1861

Born on May 7 in Kolkata, India, into a prominent Bengali family.

1901

Founded a school in Santiniketan, which later became Visva-Bharati University.

1913

Awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature for his collection of poems, Gitanjali, becoming the first non-European laureate.

1915

Knighted by the British Crown, later renounced in 1919 in protest of the Jallianwala Bagh massacre.

1919

Wrote a letter condemning the Jallianwala Bagh massacre, renouncing his knighthood as a protest against British oppression.

1921

Founded Visva-Bharati University in Santiniketan, promoting global education and cultural exchange.

1941

Passed away on August 7, leaving a legacy as a poet, philosopher, and cultural icon.

Gitanjali Poem 35

Where the mind is without fear and the head is held high;
Where knowledge is free;
Where the world has not been broken up into fragments by narrow domestic walls;
Where words come out from the depth of truth;
Where tireless striving stretches its arms towards perfection;
Where the clear stream of reason has not lost its way into the dreary desert sand of dead habit;
Where the mind is led forward by thee into ever-widening thought and action—
Into that heaven of freedom, my Father, let my country awake.

Gitanjali Poem 36

This is my prayer to thee, my lord—strike, strike at the root of penury in my heart.
Give me the strength lightly to bear my joys and sorrows.
Give me the strength to make my love fruitful in service.
Give me the strength never to disown the poor or bend my knees before insolent might.
Give me the strength to raise my mind high above daily trifles.
And give me the strength to surrender my strength to thy will with love.
